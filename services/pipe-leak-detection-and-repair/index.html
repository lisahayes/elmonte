Pipe Leak Detection & Repair in El Monte, CA

Two separate pipe systems run through every El Monte home. One carries water in under pressure. The other carries it out by gravity. They fail differently, they hide differently, and they get found differently. Knowing which side is leaking is the first fork in every diagnosis.

Pressure Side or Gravity Side: The First Question

A pressurized supply leak sprays, hisses, and runs around the clock, so it shows up on the meter and the bill fast. A drain leak only moves water while a fixture drains, which makes it slower, quieter, and often nastier, because what escapes is waste water soaking into framing or soil.

The meter test splits the two in minutes. Movement with everything off convicts the supply side. A still meter with ongoing moisture points at drains, and dye or camera testing takes over from there. Getting this fork right first saves hours of searching on the wrong system.

Materials Age on Different Clocks

El Monte homes carry a full museum of pipe. Galvanized steel supply from the pre-war blocks corrodes shut from the inside, losing pressure before it ever leaks. Postwar copper pits under hard water. ABS and PVC drains from later decades crack at fittings and get crushed by roots. Each material has a signature failure, and reading the material tells us where to look and what fix will actually last.

That is also why repairs here are rarely one-size answers. New copper spliced into crumbling galvanized just moves the next failure a foot down the line. We repair in kind where the pipe is sound and recommend material transitions where it is not, with the reasoning shown to you on the removed section.

Finding the Break Without Opening the House

Once the failing system is known, location follows the standard instrument sequence. Branch-by-branch pressure isolation corners a supply leak to one run. Listening gear and thermal imaging then put a mark on the wall, floor, or ceiling where it hides. Drain-side suspects get the camera, dye tabs, and flood testing of individual fixtures until the escape point is proven.

Failures inside walls hand off cleanly to wall leak work with the spot already marked, and buried runs continue under our underground line service with electronic locating. The point everywhere is the same: one confirmed location, one clean opening.

Small Leaks Deserve Fast Calls Too

Homeowners often sit on a minor pipe leak because it seems too small to bother anyone about. The math disagrees. A drip at one drop per second wastes roughly eleven gallons a day, every day, billed at local rates. On the drain side, a slow seep can rot a sill plate or feed mold for a season before the smell gives it away.

Small also means cheap to fix. A weeping joint caught early is often a one-hour visit. The same joint ignored through a summer can mean opened drywall, treated framing, and a repair bill several times larger. If you know about a leak, however small, calling (626) 898-6169 this week beats calling next month every time.

Pipe Leak Basics, Answered for El Monte

How do I tell a supply leak from a drain leak myself?

Run the meter test first: all water off, read the meter, wait thirty minutes, read again. Movement means a pressurized supply leak. If the meter holds still but moisture keeps appearing, run each fixture one at a time and watch for the leak to respond. A stain that grows after showers but not otherwise is telling you which drain to suspect.

Can you match repairs to my home's existing pipe?

Yes. We repair copper with copper or an approved PEX splice, galvanized with proper transition fittings that prevent dielectric corrosion, and ABS or PVC drains with the correct solvent and couplings for the material. Mixing materials without the right fittings causes early failures, and it is one of the most common defects we find in previous handyman repairs.

Is a leak in one pipe a sign the rest are failing?

Sometimes, and the removed section answers it. Pipe that failed at a mechanical point, like a fitting or a nail strike, says nothing about the rest. Pipe with wall thinning or pitting along its whole length is a system-wide warning. We show you which pattern yours has, and if it is the second kind, call (626) 898-6169 for a whole-home pressure check before the next one opens.
