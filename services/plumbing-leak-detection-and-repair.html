One Call Covers Every Leak in the House

Homeowners often delay calling because they cannot tell us what kind of leak they have. That is backwards. Identifying the leak is our half of the job. Yours is only to notice the evidence: a bill that jumped, a meter that moves overnight, a stain, a smell, a sound, a warm patch, a soft spot in the yard.

Bring us any one of those and the diagnostic sequence takes over. It makes no difference at dispatch whether the answer turns out to be a slab line, a toilet flapper, an irrigation valve, or a water heater fitting. The truck carries the tools for all of them.

How Full-System Diagnosis Works

The sequence is elimination, run in a fixed order. The meter test comes first and answers the biggest question: is water leaving the pressurized system at all? Isolating the house from irrigation splits the problem in half again. Fixture-by-fixture checks catch the silent offenders, with dye tabs exposing toilet flappers that leak without a sound.

Whatever survives elimination gets the instruments: acoustic listening for pressurized escapes, thermal imaging for hidden moisture, pressure isolation by branch to corner the loss. The process converges on one answer, usually inside a single visit, and you see each finding as it lands.

Found and Fixed by the Same Team

Detection that hands you a report and leaves invites a second contractor, a second service call, and a second explanation of everything. We repair what we find, in the same visit when parts and scope allow. Simple failures like supply lines, angle stops, and fixture connections get fixed on the spot with your approval.

Bigger findings route into the right specialty without a handoff. An under-slab break proceeds as slab leak work with the location already marked. A failed supply or drain segment becomes a scoped pipe repair with pricing in writing first. Whole-home concerns after repeat failures are covered honestly under residential leak service, including when the right answer is monitoring rather than immediate work.

When a Leak Is an Emergency, and What to Do First

Most leaks are urgent without being emergencies. Three situations skip straight to emergency handling: water flowing where you can see it and cannot stop it, any leak reaching electrical outlets or fixtures, and sewage backing into the home. In each case, act before you call. Shut the main valve, usually at the front hose bib or the meter box near the sidewalk. Kill power to any affected room at the breaker panel. Then dial (626) 898-6169 and say the word emergency, and dispatch reorders the queue.

It pays to find your main shutoff on a calm day rather than a flooded one. Walk the front of the house, find the valve, and turn it once to confirm it still moves. Older gate valves in this city can seize after decades untouched, and a seized main is worth swapping on your schedule instead of during a burst.

Everything short of those three situations still gets fast handling, usually same-day inside El Monte. The difference is only in which truck reaches you next.

General Leak Questions, Answered Straight

My water bill doubled but I see no water anywhere. Now what?

Start with the meter: shut every fixture and appliance, note the reading, wait thirty minutes without using water, and read again. Movement confirms a real leak in the pressurized system, most often under the slab, in a wall, or in the irrigation. Bring that meter result to your call and the technician arrives already knowing which tests to run first.

Do you charge to come out and diagnose?

Estimates on-site are free, and diagnostic testing is quoted before it starts, so there is no meter running on mystery time. When testing leads to repair work with us, the diagnostic cost folds into the job. You will know every number before you approve anything, in writing.

Can a leak really be silent and invisible?

Easily. A toilet flapper can pass hundreds of gallons a day in complete silence, and an under-slab pinhole can run for months with no surface sign but the bill. That invisibility is exactly why the meter test matters and why instruments beat eyeballs for finding leaks. If something feels off, trust the numbers and call (626) 898-6169.
