Detection Methods

Slab-on-grade construction across El Monte means most leaks hide under concrete. These are the non-destructive methods we use to mark a leak within inches before anything opens. Ask the dispatcher at (626) 898-6169 which method fits your symptom.

Acoustic Leak Detection

Ground microphones hear pressurized water escaping through concrete and soil, narrowing a hidden leak to inches.

Acoustic Leak Detection →

Electronic Leak Detection

Line tracing and correlation equipment maps exactly where pipes run and where the signal says water is leaving them.

Electronic Leak Detection →

Thermal Imaging Leak Detection

Infrared cameras reveal the temperature shadow of wet material behind drywall and under flooring without touching either.

Thermal Imaging Leak Detection →

Ultrasonic Leak Detection

High-frequency sensors pick up the hiss of small pressurized leaks that ground mics and meters can miss.

Ultrasonic Leak Detection →

Tracer Gas (Helium) Leak Detection

For the leaks nothing else finds: a safe helium mix charges the line and a sniffer locates where it escapes.

Tracer Gas →

Pinpoint Leak Detection

Multiple methods layered on one job to mark a leak location precisely before any concrete or drywall opens.

Pinpoint Leak Detection →

Non-Invasive Leak Detection

Every tool on the truck that finds water without cutting: acoustic, thermal, electronic, and pressure isolation together.

Non-Invasive Leak Detection →

Trenchless Leak Detection

Trenchless Leak Detection →
