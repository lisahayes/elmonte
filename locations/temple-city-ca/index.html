Where Irrigation Dominates the Outdoor Call

Irrigation systems in Temple City are serious investments, not afterthoughts. Long mainlines, multi-zone manifolds, and drip systems threading mature trees are the norm. Their failures follow the system-level logic: zone-by-zone pressure checks and valve manifold inspection beat guessing from the lawn surface. The elimination habit, always-on mains first then zone valves then laterals, locates most Temple City outdoor leaks in one visit.

Root systems from the city's mature tree canopy add their own variable: planted decades ago beside clay and iron laterals, they have had time to find every joint. Camera inspection of the lateral is worth doing before any real estate transaction on the older residential streets.

Temple City Plumbing Questions

My Temple City irrigation bill doubled this summer. Where do we start?

At the meter with the house isolated: irrigation off for a few dry days, then the house main closed while you read the meter. The sequence identifies whether the loss is in the irrigation system, the house, or both. Bill spikes in summer on irrigated lots are classic buried-lateral territory. Call (626) 898-6169 and describe the numbers you saw; the locate finds the break before anyone digs on a guess.

Are the mature trees in Temple City likely to be in my lateral?

On lots this well-planted, the answer is often yes by now. Roots find clay and iron joints reliably over decades, and Temple City's canopy is old enough to have done it. The camera inspection shows the exact condition and what the roots have reached, and the renewal options, including trenchless lining where the pipe qualifies, follow from that footage.
